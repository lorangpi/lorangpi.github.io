About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Tufts University and the Austrian Institute of Technology, working under the supervision of Professor Matthias Scheutz. My research focuses on developing neuro-symbolic architectures that enable robots to learn, adapt, and operate robustly in open-world environments characterized by novelty and unpredictability.

My work bridges symbolic reasoning and continuous control, combining the interpretability of symbolic systems with the adaptability of neural models. I aim to enable robots to perform long-horizon tasks with minimal data, adapt continuously to unseen conditions, and scale from simulation to real-world industrial applications.

Research Overview

Current Projects

Foundation Model-Assisted Robot Learning: Developing expert-free frameworks that use vision-language models to automatically discover symbolic task structures and motor skills from human demonstrations on real robotic systems.

LLM-Guided Symbolic Planning: Integrating large language models with classical symbolic planners and reinforcement learning for adaptive robot behavior in novel situations.

Industrial Applications: Deploying neuro-symbolic systems on autonomous forklifts and robotic arms in collaboration with the Austrian Institute of Technology.
